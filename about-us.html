WHAT IS UNCLAIMED MONEY

Unclaimed moneys are moneys that are forgotten or neglected by their rightful owner and has been surrendered to the Malaysian Accountant General’s account. The handling and management of unclaimed are governed by law under the Unclaimed Moneys Act 1965 (Act 370). The Unclaimed Moneys Act 1965 requires businesses entity to send to the government a report if there’s any unclaimed moneys in their possession. Unclaimed money can be in the form of :
  
DORMANT BANK
SHARE DIVIDENDS 
LIFE INSURANCE POLICIES
UNCLAIMED WAGES
EPF CONTRIBUTIONS


WHERE DOES THE MONEY COME FROM?
Bank Account - A current or savings account is declared dormant if it has not been accessed for 7 years or more and will be closed by the bank. The balance in the closed account will be submitted as unclaimed moneys to the Registrar of Unclaimed Moneys. Shares and Dividen - When company dividends that are paid out could not reach the rightful recipient the amount will be sent to the Registrars of Unclaimed Moneys. In the case of mergers and acquisitions or privatizations of listed companies, a compulsory takeover of the shares of uncontactable shareholders will result in a large pool of unclaimed moneys that are sent to the Registrar of Unclaimed Moneys.

Other assets:
Life Insurance
Wills / Estates
Unclaimed cheques
Bill Overpayments
